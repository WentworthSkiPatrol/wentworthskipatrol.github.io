Wentworth Update ~ December 10

Hello Wentworth Patrollers,

We had a very successful Lodge Day last weekend, with 57 patrollers attending (a record?) and another 5 sending their regrets. I especially enjoyed presentations from Mike Luckett of the NS Medical Call Centre on ways we are looking at to better integrate the CSP and the EHS system and from Patrick Fok on a Penthrox (pain management) trial he will be carrying out this winter. If you neither attended or let me know you couldn't make it, please fire off an email to alex.rhinelander@skipatrol.ca so I know that you are intending to patrol this winter. 

The hill is looking great, with both a natural snow covering and significant piles of man-made snow. As anyone who has seen the videos of early-season Explosion turns (videos on the NSZ FB page) can imagine, things are looking good for an early start to the year!!!

If you missed Lodge Day please carefully read this document, which has Wentworth-specific policies and procedures, and be sure to tell your team lead on the first day you patrol, so they can further review with you any needed protocols. Also see below for a few more to-dos :-). 

I am looking forward to skiing/riding with all of you, and LET IT SNOW!

Cheers,
Alex

Rostering & Training

The standard expectation for Wentworth patrollers is 5 weekend/event shifts (around 35 - 40 hours), with no more than 3 of those being Saturdays. (Note that you could do 3 Saturday shifts plus 2 Saturday events if you wished.) But we are flexible—if this doesn't work for you, reach out to Alex (alex.rhinelander@skipatrol.ca) to arrange an alternate schedule.

Passes

Wentworth is again using an online waiver and photo submission form for creating ski patrol passes. If you submit your information on this web form you will receive an email which lets you upload your preferred photo and sign the waiver. You will then be able to pick up your pass at the hill.

Please Note: Wentworth season passes are provided to patrollers who have Wentworth as their primary hill as well as patrollers who intend to patrol a significant number of days at Wentworth. If you are a member of the latter group and would like to receive a pass, please reach out to Alex in order to have your name added to the list of those eligible. 

We are happy to provide day passes to those who intend to patrol more occasionally.
