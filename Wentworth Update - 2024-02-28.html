Wentworth Update ~ 28 Feb 2024

Hello Wentworth Patrollers,

it seems crazy that Friday is March 1st, but it is! A truly awesome February is drawing to a close with a damp and windy roar, but I'm hoping that the base holds up to the weather and that once tomorrow night's freeze has hit there will be enough snow left for the groomers to work their magic on.

Huge thanks to those who answered the Brunner call: Friday was well-staffed and the hill had its first top-to-bottom Super-G course in years. Then, after weather cancelled Saturday's races, everyone came together and supported the racers in running both Slalom and GS on Sunday: 116 racers in four starts over two race courses. Weather and snow conditions were perfect, and all went off without a hitch. Again, many thanks to all and especially to Mike Huggins for leading the charge. 

On the horizon: the Atlantic Division On Patrol Clinic is on for this Saturday and Sunday (Meet & Greet Friday evening in Ducky's—feel free to show up and crash the party!). Then comes March Break the week of March 11 – 15. All help during that period is highly appreciated. 

If you have not yet managed to get your hours in, there is still time! We're a little thin this weekend (four signed up for Saturday and four for Sunday) and there are still a few more events. Sign up before it's too late!!!

I look forward to seeing you On Snow,

Alex Rhinelander 
Ski Wentworth Patrol Leader
alex.rhinelander@skipatrol.ca

Atlantic East On Patrol Clinic

Friday, Mar 1:  

  • 19:30 – Meet & Greet @ Ducky's 

Saturday, Mar 2:  

  • 09:00 – 12:00: Ski/Ride & Pre-Toboggan Skills 
    This session is intended to let people work on the fundamental skills in the CSP On-Snow curriculum with a focus on the skills that provide a solid foundation for toboggan handling.  
  • 08:30 – 12:00: Review of On-Snow Skills for Instructors
    This session is for the On-Snow Instructors to answer questions and review the finer points of On-Snow Skills for both skiers and snowboarders. 
  • 12:30 – 16:30: On-Snow Scenario Showcase
    Work on a series of first aid and rescue scenarios on the hill as part of a team.  The teams will be announced at the Friday evening Meet & Greet.  You could call this a “First Aid Competition” but don’t stress too much about it. 😉 The goal is to learn from each other, pick up some tips and tricks, and have fun!  
    19:00 – late: Food, BEvERages, awards, and prizes
    Celebrate the team who totally rocked the On-Snow Scenario Showcase and get yourself some sweet door prizes. 

Sunday, Mar 3:  

  • 08:30 – 11:00: Managing Critically Ill Patients in a toboggan
    This session will focus on working through some strategies for managing critically ill patients, based on the concepts outlined in Appendix A “CPR in a toboggan” of the On Snow manual.  
  • 11:00 – 12:00: Review of On-Snow Skills for Instructors (continued) 
  • 13:00 – 16:30: Basics of Rope Work - awareness
    This session will review some ways that rope work could be incorporated into rescue scenarios in the ski patrol environment. The goal is to give people a taste of rope work. If you walk away from this session hungry for more, consider talking to your Patrol Leader about the activities and training appropriate to your hill. This session will not qualify you to perform any rope rescue techniques. Proper training is required before you do any of this “in the wild”.

Rostering & Training

The standard expectation for Wentworth patrollers is 5 weekend/event shifts (around 35 - 40 hours), with no more than 3 of those being Saturdays. (Note that you could do 3 Saturday shifts plus 2 Saturday events if you wished.) But we are flexible—if this doesn't work for you, reach out to Alex (alex.rhinelander@skipatrol.ca) to arrange an alternate schedule.

Training will commence with an instructors-only session the morning of Jan 6 followed by new patroller training that afternoon. Following the 6th, instructors will be able to sign up to provide training whenever they will be available. 
Radio Procedures

At Wentworth, calls related to first aid or patroller availability should now be made to “Ski Patrol Dispatch.”

On weekend days before sweep, these calls are answered
  1. by the patroller on bump, or
  2. by the Team Lead, if there is no bump.

On nights and weekdays, Mountain Safety will answer these calls. The time of all calls related to incidents are logged by a lift attendant at the top of one of the chair lifts.

When initiating a call, ALWAYS say the name of the station you are calling first, followed by ‘this is’ and your own name, e.g. “Ski Patrol Dispatch, this is patroller John.”

For more details and some sample conversations, see the document Wentworth: CSP Protocols.
