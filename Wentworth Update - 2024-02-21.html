Wentworth Update ~ 21 Feb 2024

Hello Wentworth Patrollers,

February is always a busy month, but this February's snowfall has made it especially so. The hill has been humming with lots of guests and lots of events. My thanks to all who have stepped up and contributed‚Äîthere were 112 patroller sign-ins over the five days of the past two weekends! Our efforts are very much appreciated by the hill. I received the following note from Leslie on Tuesday.

Hi Alex

I wanted to reach out and personally thank the Wentworth CSP members for the past two weeks. They were the busiest extended weekends we‚Äôve had at Wentworth since I‚Äôve been in my position, over 20 years.

CSP members responded to many calls around the mountain from glades and the creek to rental shop and bunny hill. I was so impressed with the level of professionalism and commitment.

And to make things even more hectic, we called on CSP for two alpine ski races, two freestyle ski competitions and three snowboard. All went off without a hitch!

Please express my gratitude to members who volunteered their personal time these past two weeks to help us put our systems to the max. Everyone came together and made it a positive ski and snowboard experience for our guests. What a success!

Thank you,
Leslie

We're not quite out of the woods yet, however: there are two more busy weekends before things settle back down to a more relaxed pace. The Rolland Brunner races are scheduled for Friday‚ÄìSunday this coming weekend, and both the Atlantic Division On Patrol clinic and the FNS Moguls event are scheduled the following weekend. Given that we have 20 Wentworth patrollers signed up to participate in the clinic, we will need everyone available to rally and come out to provide hill & event coverage. Please sign up!

A few quick reminders:

  • Parking tickets - The lots have filled up several times over the past few busy weekend days, and the overflow of cars has parked along the sides of Highway 4. The police have been by a few times, and although they have so far (AFAIK) been only handing out warnings, they could switch to ticketing without notice. Tip: If you come early, you will get a legal spot!

  • Sign OUT - We have been very good about signing IN in the sign-in book, but some patrollers are less consistent with their sign OUTs. Please sign out so (1) we know that you are safely off the hill, and (2) I can accurately calculate our patrol hours. Thanks!

  • Support the chairloader - It is critically important to support the toboggan chairloader so it cannot drop down and unhook itself. Normally, that support is provided by the Sun Valley splint packed into the toboggan. If, however, you bring down an empty toboggan (e.g. on a taxi ride), you MUST place something under the chair loader before loading on the charlift. If your first aid kit won't do the trick, consider bringing the blanket bag down with you on the taxi ride. 

Once more, thank you all for everything you do!

I look forward to seeing you On Snow,

Alex Rhinelander 
Ski Wentworth Patrol Leader

Rostering & Training

The standard expectation for Wentworth patrollers is 5 weekend/event shifts (around 35 - 40 hours), with no more than 3 of those being Saturdays. (Note that you could do 3 Saturday shifts plus 2 Saturday events if you wished.) But we are flexible‚Äîif this doesn't work for you, reach out to Alex (alex.rhinelander@skipatrol.ca) to arrange an alternate schedule.

Training will commence with an instructors-only session the morning of Jan 6 followed by new patroller training that afternoon. Following the 6th, instructors will be able to sign up to provide training whenever they will be available. 
Radio Procedures

At Wentworth, calls related to first aid or patroller availability should now be made to ‚ÄúSki Patrol Dispatch.‚Äù

On weekend days before sweep, these calls are answered
  1. by the patroller on bump, or
  2. by the Team Lead, if there is no bump.

On nights and weekdays, Mountain Safety will answer these calls. The time of all calls related to incidents are logged by a lift attendant at the top of one of the chair lifts.

When initiating a call, ALWAYS say the name of the station you are calling first, followed by ‚Äòthis is‚Äô and your own name, e.g. ‚ÄúSki Patrol Dispatch, this is patroller John.‚Äù

For more details and some sample conversations, see the document Wentworth: CSP Protocols.
