Wentworth Update ~ 15 January 2025

Hello Wentworth Patrollers,

Not too much from me this week, but a good number of items below, so please read on. 

One thing I will say is that a few people have asked me to confirm new policies or procedures that they have 'heard from a few people.' In each case, the rumours that were relayed to me were not accurate. We follow:

  1. CSP policies as published in the AFA and On Patrol manuals
  2. Wentworth-specific policies as published in Wentworth CSP Protocols (the most recent version is linked to on SUG)

Please review these three documents! If any changes need to be made, they will be communicated to patrollers during Zone-approved training, by Josh (as head of Mountain Safety) or me (as Patrol Leader). Wentworth-specific changes may be mentioned at a morning meeting, but they will also be sent to the patrol in general via this Update and will be added to the Wentworth CSP Protocols document ASAP. 

See you on snow,
Alex

Incident Forms

Incident forms are generally pretty good, but a gentle reminder to include the following key items: name, age, community, phone number, gender, season or day pass, incident location (be specific, 'Rosebowl' is a lot of terrain), patroller notes (what did you do), rental equipment or guest's own, how did they arrive/leave the hut and what is the reported destination (home/hospital/more skiing).

You MUST legibly record the patient's name and phone number (or the parent/guarian's if they are a minor)!!!

~Josh
Uniforms

Just a reminder that you must fill out and sign the loan agreement if you are borrowing a jacket for the season. The agreements can be found in the white binder in the Patrol Hut. Look for a blank one and insert it in the 2024-25 section. 

~Rick
FA Kits

The bum bags and vests in the Patrol Hut are for daily use only. Be sure to check the contents before patrolling with a borrowed pack, and thanks for leaving it in good shape for the next person who borrows it for the day!

As there are usually only ten bumbags/vests available, it is suggested that each patroller have their own FA kit. New patrollers, feel free to borrow kits until you settle on which style suits you. 

One Wentworth-specific thing to keep in mind is that Wentworth patrollers are currently allowed to ride the chairlifts without removing backpacks if those packs are low-profile and have no dangling loops or straps. Before investing in a backpack, you may wish to verify that it would qualify and be aware that while hill management has signed off on this policy, it could change. 

All Ferno orders (i.e. orders for these items) must go through national. Reach out to Rick Crooks at rick.crooks@skipatrol.ca to place that order.

Rostering & Training

The standard expectation for Wentworth patrollers is 5 weekend/event shifts (around 35 - 40 hours), with no more than 3 of those being Saturdays. (Note that you could do 3 Saturday shifts plus 2 Saturday events if you wished.) But we are flexible—if this doesn't work for you, reach out to Alex (alex.rhinelander@skipatrol.ca) to arrange an alternate schedule.
