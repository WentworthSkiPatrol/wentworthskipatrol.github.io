Wentworth Update ~ 1 January 2025

Hello Wentworth Patrollers,

Happy New Year!

Huge thanks to all those patrollers who managed to get to the hill over the holidays—they were rewarded by excellent early-season conditions and their time and efforts were much appreciated. Thanks, too, to Rick Crooks and all the instructors who have embarked on OnSnow training and requals. If you are in need of training, be sure to sign up for a training slot on SUG. 

The Nova Scotia weather yo-yo is back at it, with an unpleasant-looking first two days of the new year. Starting Friday, however, there is a two-week run of cold weather forecast that should let the hill make lots of snow and continue opening up new trails. 

See you On Snow,
Alex

Rostering & Training

The standard expectation for Wentworth patrollers is 5 weekend/event shifts (around 35 - 40 hours), with no more than 3 of those being Saturdays. (Note that you could do 3 Saturday shifts plus 2 Saturday events if you wished.) But we are flexible—if this doesn't work for you, reach out to Alex (alex.rhinelander@skipatrol.ca) to arrange an alternate schedule.
SignUpGenius
  • SUG is now open for patrollers to sign up for their 5 shifts, with Patrol, Event and Training slots all posted.
  • Everything—rosters, training, competitions, socials—is on the same signup
  • There are 10 patrol slots each day, but we really want 6-7 patrollers on weekends and 2 on weekdays
  • Sign-ups are most useful when made well in advance.
