Wentworth Update ~ 8 November 2023

Hello Wentworth Patrollers,

Firstly, huge thanks are due to Rick Crooks and every one of his team of Instructors and ITs who have gotten us all where we are. The number of hours that are involved in organizing, training themselves, and then providing training to the rest of us is incredible. Kudos to them all!

Secondly, congratulations and thanks to Paul Saulnier for organizing a fantastic Awards Gala. It was a fun night and I had a great time meeting new patrollers and catching up to friends from around the Zone. 

And thirdly, speaking of new patrollers, a warm welcome to our new and new-to-Wentworth patrollers. I'm very glad you have joined us and look forward to patrolling together.

Please note two important upcoming events. The Wentworth Ski Swap is the weekend after next (see below for more details and a link to sign up), and Lodge Day is in just over one month, on Sunday, December 10th. More details to follow in the next Wentworth Update.

Cheers,
Alex

Ski Swap

This year's Wentworth Ski and Snowboard Swap is Saturday, November 18th. Setup and equipment drop off are in the afternoon and evening of Friday, November 17th. We will be inside on the main level of the Wentworth Lodge.

We are asking for your support of this important fundraiser by signing up for one or more shifts. If you plan to help with the swap, we ask that you please use Signup Genius, as this helps us plan staffing.

Passes

Wentworth is again using an online waiver and photo submission form for creating ski patrol passes. If you submit your information on this web form you will receive an email which lets you upload your preferred photo and sign the waiver. You will then be able to pick up your pass at the hill.

Name Badges

All name badge orders must be sent in by the Zone to National, and our order will be going in on November 14th. If you wish place an order, please do so by sending the information listed below to Mahmoud Ahrari (mahmoud.ahrari@skipatrol.ca) before that date. 

  1. The type of badge. Large badges fit Avalanche jackets and Action Rescue vests, small ones fit Helly Hanson jackets and vests.
  2. Your name. The NS Zone uses first name only, but you may choose to use the name you are know by (e.g. my badge reads Alex not Alexander). 
  3. NEW-Badges may now include your preferred pronouns (see below). 
  4. The quantity of badges you would like. 

Members now have the option to include their chosen pronouns (he/him; she/her; they/them) at no additional cost to their name badge. Please ensure this point is highlighted to your patrol teams so that new candidates can include, or returning patrollers can choose to purchase an updated name badge.
